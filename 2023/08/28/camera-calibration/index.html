Motivation

I have found that there have been some difference of calibrating result. The calibrated optical center from the same camera of one calibration algorithm varies about 5-7 pixels. In the following article, this problem is called "the optical center pesudo-displacement problem". In addition, I also found that the calibrating result is not robust with respect to the slight difference of camera placement. From an intuitive view, I think the common difference of optical center is in 0.5 pixels. I think there is a large optimization space to improve the calibration algorithm.

Corner detection of the Chessboard

Corner Point Detection is the first step to detect corner points on the image. The chessboard is placed in front of camera.
