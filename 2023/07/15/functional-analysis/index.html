Cardinal Numbers

How to count a set with inifinite elements? Such as the number of rational numbers and irational numbers.

Given a relation R\mathcal{R}R, which means "there exists a bijection of AAA onto BBB", where AAA and BBB are the subsets of XXX. Such a relation is a equivalence relation R\mathcal{R}R on set P.\mathcal{P}.P.

The elements of quotient set P(X)/R⊂P(P(X))\mathcal{P}(X)/R \subset \mathcal{P}(\mathcal{P}(X))P(X)/R⊂P(P(X)) are called cardinal numbers of the subsets of XXX. If AAA is the subset of XXX, its caridnal number, denoted as

cardAcard A cardA

We can know cardinal number is not a number like Z\mathbb{Z}Z, R\mathbb{R}R and C\mathbb{C}C. It just pick out all the subset of XXX with the same "size".

Vector Space

A set XXX is called a vector space over K\mathbb{K}K when it support addition or scalar(complex or real) multiplication.

A subspace of a vector space is any subset of XXX that is also a vector space.

A direct sum of subspace Y,ZY,ZY,Z of space XXX if any $x \in X $ can be written down as
